## Core Philosophy (Always Apply)

**The Hiring Equation** (Langstaff's foundational principle):
> If (Their Requirements) = (Your Experience) → You Qualify

Three things must happen to land any job:
1. **Uncover requirements** — dissect the job posting for keywords, skills, and behaviors
2. **Show you meet them** — via resume, cover letter, LinkedIn, and interview answers
3. **Earn their trust** — networking, referrals, and social proof close the gap

**Mindset**: Resume = marketing document. You = the product. Every word exists to answer: *"Should we buy this product?"*

**5-Second Rule**: Hiring managers scan resumes for 5–10 seconds before deciding. Every section must survive that scan.

## The Job Posting Dissection (Always Do This First)

Before touching any document, extract two categories from the job posting:

| Extract | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| **Job Duties & Skills** | Tasks, tools, technologies, credentials, achievements they want |
| **Work Behaviors** | Traits: resilient, collaborative, fast-paced, customer-obsessed |

These become your **keyword master list** — the spine of everything you write.

**ChatGPT shortcut** (from `references/07-chatgpt-prompts.md` → Prompt 1+2):
Load 2–3 job descriptions, ask ChatGPT to extract the top 25 skills. This builds your Areas of Expertise section instantly.

## Resume: The 10/10 Template Structure

Full detail in `references/01-resume-architecture.md`. Summary:

```
[NAME]
[Professional Title 1] | [Professional Title 2]

PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY (3–4 sentences, third person)
CAREER HIGHLIGHTS (2–3 labeled accomplishments)
AREAS OF EXPERTISE / KEY SKILLS (10–16 skills, populated last)

PROFESSIONAL EXPERIENCE
  Company | Title | Location | Month YYYY – Month YYYY
  2–3 line job overview (context only, no accomplishments)
  • Badass Bullet Point (Action Verb + Specific Detail + Result)
  • Badass Bullet Point
  ...

EDUCATION
```

**The 5-5-5 Method** (quick refresh for existing resumes):
1. Add a professional title under your name
2. Add numbers to every bullet
3. Bold the most impressive phrase in each bullet

## Bullet Points: The Core Formula

Full detail + all 6 formulas in `references/02-bullet-formulas.md`.

**Langstaff's 3-Part Structure** (always):
```
[Action Verb] + [Specific / Quantifiable Detail] + [Result]
```

**Bad**: "Sold automotive insurance."
**Badass**: "Connected with 30–40 prospective customers per day, understanding their unique needs and offering tailored automotive insurance solutions. Consistently achieved 110% of monthly sales quota."

**If you don't have hard numbers**, use: scope/scale, before/after framing, proxy metrics, process creation/"firsts", recognition signals, ranges/estimates, or cost calculations.

## ATS: The Non-Negotiable Rules

Full detail in `references/03-ats-optimization.md`. The five most critical:

1. **Single-column layout only** — multi-column breaks 39%+ of ATS systems
2. **No graphics, text boxes, tables, or images** — they're invisible to parsers
3. **Standard section headings** — "Work Experience" not "My Journey"
4. **DOCX or text-based PDF** — never .pages, .odt, or image-based PDFs
5. **75–80% keyword match** to the job description (use Jobscan to verify)

**The Notepad Test**: Paste resume into Notepad. If the text is clean and in order, ATS can read it.

## Cover Letter: The 5-Minute Framework

Full templates in `references/04-cover-letters.md`.

**Structure**: Branding → Address → Intro (3–5 sentences) → 4–6 Bullet Points → Closing → Sign-off

**Why bullet points in a cover letter?** They're scannable, undeniable, and far easier to write than paragraphs. Langstaff has used this approach for hundreds of clients with consistent success.

**Bullet types**: Specific Example (strongest) > Summary of Experience > "This Is Important to Me" (once max)

## LinkedIn: The Core Four

Full detail in `references/05-linkedin-optimization.md`.

Recruiters decide in 1–2 seconds based on four things:
1. **Photo** — professional, direct eye contact, solid background
2. **Banner** — branded (build in Canva), not the default gradient
3. **Headline** — 220 chars: `[Role] | [Value Prop] | [Key Skills] | [Differentiator]`
4. **About** — first 3 lines must hook before "See more" truncation

All-Star profile = 40x more opportunities. 5+ skills listed = 33x more recruiter contact.

## Interview Prep

Full detail in `references/06-interview-prep.md`.

**Prediction system**: Dissect job posting → turn each keyword into a "Tell me about a time…" question → plan which career story answers each. Aim for 15–30 predicted questions before any interview.

**Answer structure**: STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) or CARL (Context, Action, Result, Learning).

**Answer length**: 1–3 minutes per answer. End by circling back to the question — never trail off with "And… yeah."

## When the User Provides Their Resume / Documents

When a user pastes or uploads their resume, cover letter, or LinkedIn profile:
1. Identify the job they're targeting (ask if not provided)
2. Dissect the job posting against their documents
3. Flag: missing keywords, weak/duty-focused bullets, missing quantification, poor branding sections
4. Suggest specific rewrites using the formulas above
5. Offer ChatGPT prompts from `references/07-chatgpt-prompts.md` for any section they want to rebuild

## AI Words to Remove Immediately

These signal that a robot wrote the document. Remove on sight:
> Meticulously, Pioneered, Prowess, Realm, Helm, Spearheaded (overused), Innovative (vague),
> Energetic, Team player, Self-starter, Good communicator, Results-driven (without numbers)
