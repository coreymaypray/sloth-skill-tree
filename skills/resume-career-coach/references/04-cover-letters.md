# Badass Cover Letters — Complete Reference (Langstaff System)

## Why You Still Need One Every Time

| Reason | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Avoids the Easy Elimination Round** | Hiring managers look for any reason to eliminate candidates from 100+ applications. Missing a requested cover letter = instant disqualification for "inability to follow instructions." |
| **Top candidates get their letters read** | After narrowing to top 5–10 candidates, hiring managers do read cover letters. Being a top candidate means having one. |
| **Demonstrates written communication** | Most jobs require writing skills. A cover letter is an instant evaluation. |
| **Traditional managers still expect it** | You don't know who's reviewing. Better safe than sorry. |
| **No risk with the right system** | Using the 5-minute framework below, each letter takes minimal time. |

---

## The 6-Part Anatomy of a Badass Cover Letter

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **1. Branding** | Name, contact info, design matching your resume. Consistency = professional brand. |
| **2. Address** | Date, company address, addressee. Use actual hiring manager name if findable. |
| **3. Intro Paragraph** | 3–5 sentences: state the role, why you applied, why you'd be great, introduce the bullets. |
| **4. Body Bullet Points** | 4–6 bullets. Unconventional but highly effective — scannable, undeniable, easier to write. |
| **5. Closing Paragraph** | Company-specific interest, thank them, call to action. |
| **6. Sign Off** | "Sincerely" + name. No physical signature needed. |

**Three inviolable rules:**
1. One page maximum. Always.
2. Specific to the job — mention job title AND company name at least twice each
3. Easily scannable — no 4–5 large blocks of text

---

## Addressing the Cover Letter

- **Best:** Use the actual hiring manager's name (LinkedIn or company website)
- **Fine:** "Dear Hiring Manager" for most roles
- **Good for senior roles:** "Dear Selection Committee"
- **Never:** "To Whom It May Concern" — too impersonal / old school

## Five Intro Paragraph Templates

### #1 — Greg's Go-To (Most Recommended for Any Role)
```
I am excited to submit my application for this [JOB TITLE] position at [COMPANY NAME].
I found this job posting online and after comparing the responsibilities to my own skills
and interests, I jumped at the chance to apply. I believe my [#] years' experience in
[JOB TITLE] have allowed me to develop the critical skills needed to excel in this position.

In my resume, as well as the page below, I have taken the time to highlight some of my
specific experiences that have prepared me to be a strong contributor to your team in
this [JOB TITLE] position.
```

### #2 — The Enthusiastic
```
I am beyond thrilled to submit my application for this incredible [JOB TITLE] opportunity
at [COMPANY NAME]. I have been searching for an opportunity like this for months and the
moment I saw this, I knew I had to apply. I wholeheartedly believe that my professional
background and education in [INDUSTRY] have prepared me to be an exceptional contributor
to your team and I have taken the time to elaborate on that below.
```

### #3 — Highly Professional
```
I am writing to formally submit my application for the [JOB TITLE] position at [COMPANY NAME].
Enclosed you will find my resume which details my [#] years of progressive experience in
[INDUSTRY] where I had the opportunity to develop the essential skills required to thrive
in this role. Please see the bullet points below as illustrations of my relevant capabilities
which have positioned me to add substantial value to your organization in this [JOB TITLE] position.
```

### #4 — Referencing a Referral
```
I am excited to submit my application for this [JOB TITLE] position at [COMPANY NAME].
A former colleague and friend of mine, [REFEREE NAME], who works with your organization as
a [THEIR JOB TITLE], recommended that I apply to this position, and I am so pleased
[HE/SHE/THEY] did because this role seems like an excellent fit. I believe my [#] years'
experience in [JOB TITLE] have allowed me to develop the critical skills needed to excel
in this position and I have elaborated on my relevant skillsets below.
```

### #5 — Internal Application
```
I am excited at the prospect of advancing my career here at [COMPANY NAME] in a new
capacity as the [JOB TITLE]. As you are aware, I have spent the last [#] years developing
an extensive knowledge of our processes and building exceptional relationships with our
incredible teams. I believe that foundation, combined with my highly relevant professional
skillset (detailed below), makes me an outstanding choice for this role.
```

## Selecting Your 4–6 Bullet Topics

Process:
1. Read the job posting and list every hard skill, soft skill, technology, and industry knowledge area mentioned
2. Ask yourself: *"If I were hiring for this job, which of these would be most important to me?"*
3. Ask yourself: *"Which of these do I have the most impressive experience with?"*
4. The intersection = your 4–6 bullet topics

---

## Three Types of Cover Letter Bullets

### Type 1 — Specific Example (Strongest — use most often)
One time you used the skill and nailed it.
```
Cost Savings | In my Operations Manager role with ABC Company, I restructured employee
workflow to reduce production bottlenecks, decreasing cost per unit by 14%.
```

### Type 2 — Summary of Experience
You're good at it because you've done it for years.
```
Staff Leadership | I have 14+ years of experience directing cross-functional teams of
10–25 staff to execute projects and day-to-day operations in high-stakes hospitality settings.
```

### Type 3 — "This Is Important to Me" (Use sparingly — max once per letter)
When you're at a loss for specific examples on a critical skill.
```
Customer Service | I place an exceptionally high priority on customer service in my work.
By building trust with customers and providing exceptional service, I have been able to
consistently retain customers at a high rate.
```

**Format tip:** Left-align the skill label, then the description to its right. Creates clean scannable layout.

## Four Closing Paragraph Templates

### #1 — Go-To (Recommended)
```
Finally, I am thrilled at the prospect of joining such an impressive organization.
[COMPANY NAME] has a great reputation and strong values that closely align with my own.
I would be grateful for the chance to contribute to your future success. Thank you very
much for reviewing my application and I look forward to further communication.
```

### #2 — Enthusiastic
```
Before wrapping up this cover letter, I want to reiterate what an exciting opportunity
this is. I have always dreamed of working for [COMPANY NAME] and I am so enthused by the
prospect of realizing that dream. You do outstanding work, and I would be incredibly proud
to contribute to your continued success. Thank you very much for reviewing my application.
```

### #3 — Highly Professional
```
Finally, I wanted to share my immense respect for your organization. You do important work,
and I would be honored to attach my reputation to the [COMPANY NAME] name. I appreciate
your time in reviewing my application, and I hope to be hearing from you shortly regarding
next steps in the process.
```

### #4 — Internal Position
```
Finally, I would like to reiterate how important [COMPANY NAME] has been to me. I have
grown so much in this organization and the prospect of contributing to our success in this
new capacity while continuing to grow as a professional is incredibly exciting. Thank you
very much for considering my application and I look forward to connecting around next steps.
```

## The Bullet Point Library Strategy

After writing each cover letter, copy every bullet you wrote into a separate **"Cover Letter Bullet Point Library"** document.

By the time you've applied to 5–10 jobs, you can build entire cover letters by copy-pasting from your own library rather than writing from scratch. A 30-minute task becomes 5 minutes.

Organize by skill category (Leadership, Budget Management, Client Relations, etc.).

## The Full Writing Checklist

- [ ] Template selected and matches resume branding
- [ ] Intro and closing paragraphs match in style and tone
- [ ] Job posting reviewed; all key traits identified
- [ ] Bullet topics selected (4–6 skills matching JD + your experience)
- [ ] Bullets written (Specific Example, Summary, or Importance)
- [ ] Bullets added to Bullet Point Library
- [ ] Job title mentioned twice; company name mentioned twice
- [ ] One page maximum confirmed
- [ ] Signed off (Sincerely + typed name)
- [ ] Proofread (yourself + at least one other read-through)
