# Bullet Point Formulas — Complete Reference

## The Universal 3-Part Structure (Langstaff)

Every great bullet point has exactly three components:

| Component | Role | Example Content |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| **Action Verb(s)** | Opens the bullet. Grabs attention, signals authority. | "Connected with," "Managed," "Implemented" |
| **Specific/Quantifiable Detail** | The bulk. Paints a specific picture. Include numbers. | "30–40 prospective customers per day" |
| **Result** | Rounds out the story. Shows success. | "Consistently achieved 110% of monthly quota" |

**Bad** → "Sold automotive insurance."
**Badass** → "Connected with 30–40 prospective customers per day, understanding their unique needs and offering tailored automotive insurance solutions. Consistently achieved 110% of monthly sales quota."

> Two sentences in a single bullet is fine. There are very few hard rules in resume writing.

## The Bullet Point Worksheet (Use Before Writing)

Fill this out BEFORE writing each bullet — prevents writer's block:

| Column | Fill In |
|--------|---------|
| Bullet Topic | What skill/achievement is this about? (e.g., "Content Creation / Social Media") |
| Action Verbs | 3–5 options (e.g., Created, Led, Curated, Planned) |
| Specific Detail | What exactly? How many? What tools? What context? |
| Result | What happened because of this? Revenue? Efficiency? Recognition? |

**Discovery questions when stuck:**
- What steps were involved in this accomplishment?
- Who were you collaborating with?
- What software, tools, or systems were you using?
- How many? How much? How often? Over what time period?

## The Six Major Formulas

### Formula 1: Langstaff's Badass Bullet (Action + Specific Detail + Result)
**Best for:** All levels, universal applicability.
```
[Action Verb] + [specific detail with numbers] + [result].
```
> "Managed social media presence across 4 platforms for 80K+ followers, creating original content calendar and growing engagement 31% YoY."

---

### Formula 2: XYZ (Google's Standard — Laszlo Bock)
**Best for:** Tech, sales, and data-driven roles with clear metrics.
```
Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z].
```
> "Reduced customer churn by 23% (X) as measured by 90-day retention data (Y) by implementing a proactive outreach program that contacted at-risk accounts within 48 hours of usage drops (Z)."

---

### Formula 3: CAR (Challenge → Action → Result)
**Best for:** Senior roles; frames you as a problem-solver, not a task-doer.
```
[Challenge/context] → [Actions taken] → [Result achieved].
```
> "Increased ROI for IT spend by 65% through elimination of low-priority projects after establishing cross-functional steering committees to align IT and business stakeholders."

---

### Formula 4: PAR (Problem → Action → Result) — and its flip RAP
**Best for:** Concise storytelling. Lead with result (RAP) when the number is impressive.
```
PAR: [Problem] → [Action] → [Result]
RAP: [Result] achieved by [Action] that addressed [Problem]
```
> RAP: "Resolved hundreds of client escalations, leading to 40% increase in client retention, by implementing a structured escalation protocol and dedicated account recovery team."

---

### Formula 5: STAR (Situation → Task → Action → Result)
**Best for:** Detailed senior bullets; compress S+T into brief context, focus depth on A+R.
```
[Brief context] → [Specific actions taken] → [Quantified result].
```
> "Managed inventory for high-volume retail store; implemented automated tracking system and collaborated with vendors to streamline procurement, reducing out-of-stock incidents by 30% and excess inventory by 20%."

---

### Formula 6: CARL (Context → Action → Result → Learning)
**Best for:** Interview answers, and senior bullets that showcase growth mindset.
```
[Context] → [Action] → [Result] → [What you learned and applied].
```
Primarily used in interviews — see `references/06-interview-prep.md`.

## Quantifying When You Don't Have Hard Numbers

Seven proven techniques:

| Technique | How to Apply | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Scope/Scale** | Team size, budget, volume, geography | "Led team of 15 across 5 business units" |
| **Before/After** | Cut the gap from X to Y | "Cut response time from 48 hours to 12 hours" |
| **Proxy Metrics** | Borrow adjacent data | "Attendance as proxy for program adoption" |
| **Process Creation** | "First ever" / "from scratch" | "Built company's first data governance framework" |
| **Recognition Signal** | Selection, promotion, award | "Selected by CEO to present to board" |
| **Ranges/Estimates** | Approximate ranges are fine | "Supervised 7–12 students each year" |
| **Cost Calculations** | Small efficiency × frequency | "Saving $200K–$350K/year through inspection streamline" |

## Bullet Language by Career Level

**The single biggest differentiator between levels is language, not just content.**

### Mid-Level (Manager / Senior IC)
- Focus: Individual contributions, project metrics
- Language: Implemented, Developed, Managed, Coordinated, Improved
- Example: "Implemented automated inventory system, reducing out-of-stock incidents by 30%."

### Director
- Focus: Bridge tactical and strategic; team and budget accountability
- Language: Led, Directed, Established, Launched, Drove, Accelerated
- Example: "Created 5-year marketing transformation strategy approved by Board; achieved 10% growth vs. -5% industry trend."

### VP / SVP
- Focus: P&L ownership, enterprise-wide impact, major initiatives
- Language: Spearheaded, Forged, Transformed, Scaled, Reinvented
- Example: "Steered $50M portfolio to record 9% EBIT uplift through operational redesign spanning 3 business units."

### C-Suite
- Focus: Transformation narratives, board interaction, shareholder value, M&A
- Language: Orchestrated, Architected, Catalyzed, Stewarded, Championed
- Example: "Championed merger integration for two Fortune 500 companies, achieving 95% employee retention and $200M in synergies within 18 months."

## AI Red Flag Words — Remove Immediately

The following words in resumes or cover letters signal AI authorship to savvy reviewers. Remove on sight:

> **Meticulously, Pioneered, Prowess, Realm, Helm, Fostered (overused), Spearheaded (overused),
> Innovative (without evidence), Energetic, Team player, Self-starter, Good communicator,
> Results-driven (without numbers), Passionate (without context), Dynamic, Exceptional**

Korn Ferry CEO Gary Burnison: *"Never describe yourself as 'innovative,' 'energetic,' 'a team player,' 'a self-starter,' or 'a good communicator.' These are so overused they have become meaningless."*

## The "So What?" Test

Before finalizing any bullet, run it through three filters:
1. **What did this accomplish?** (Is there a result?)
2. **What impact did it have on team / company?** (Is there scale?)
3. **What does it say about you?** (Does it reveal a skill or trait they want?)

If a bullet fails all three, rewrite it. If it fails one, see if you can add that element.
