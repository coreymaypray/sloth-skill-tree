# Resume Architecture — The 10/10 Template (Deep Reference)

## What Makes a Template Effective

Four criteria (Langstaff):
- **Easy to read at a glance** — hiring managers look for 5–10 seconds
- **Visually consistent** — mismatched indentation/fonts signal lack of attention to detail
- **Visually pleasing** — eye-catching AND professional (not a graphic design portfolio)
- **ATS compliant** — graphics and text boxes break automated parsing

> "The words on your resume are 10x more important than the design." — Langstaff

---

## Section 1: Name + Dual Professional Titles

Under your name, place **two professional titles**. This is Langstaff's #1 branding technique — answers "What are you?" within 2 seconds.

**Four types of titles:**

| Type | When to Use | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Your actual job title | Applying for same type of role | `Forensic Accountant` |
| Summary of expertise | Title doesn't match perfectly | `Seasoned Accounting Professional` |
| Enthusiasm modifier | Less experience / recent grad | `Marketing Enthusiast`, `Data Specialist` |
| General strong trait | Second title, or no industry match | `Relationship-Oriented Leader`, `Innovative Problem Solver` |

**Dual title examples:**
```
Sarah Mitchell
Digital Marketing Strategist | Relationship-Focused Leader

James Carter
SaaS Sales Expert | Innovative Problem Solver

Dr. Priya Nair
Healthcare Operations Executive | Transformation-Driven Leader
```

## Section 2: Professional Summary (3–4 Sentences, Third Person)

**The formula:**

| Sentence | Content |
|----------|---------|
| 1 | `[Positive adjective] [title] with [X] years of experience [doing 2 key things from job posting]` |
| 2 | `[2–3 more things from job posting they've effectively done] + optional generic result` |
| 3 | Values: `Passionate about...` or `Highly values...` using cultural language from the posting |
| 4 (optional) | `Committed to continuous improvement and team success` or similar |

**Example — Operations Manager:**
> "Results-oriented operations manager with 12 years' experience driving improvements to impact bottom-line success in the tile and flooring industry. Confident in ability to manage teams of 30+ staff in a fast-paced warehouse setting. Committed to continuous improvement and impacting team success."

**Rules:**
- Never write in first person ("I am..." → wrong)
- 3–5 lines on the page max
- Keywords from job posting should appear naturally

## Section 3: Career Highlights

2–3 short, labeled accomplishments that front-load your top selling points.

**Two styles:**

**Style A — Summary of Experience:**
```
Team Management: 4+ years managing digital marketing teams in high-stakes, fast-paced environments.
```

**Style B — Specific Accomplishment:**
```
B2B Business Development: Prospected and signed 100+ new accounts, grew revenue by 120% in 2017.
```

**Sample block:**
```
CAREER HIGHLIGHTS
Efficiency Increase: Shifted warehouse processing to enable 10% reduction in delivery time.
Cost Savings: Negotiated 15% cost reduction with material supplier, saving company $1.4M/year.
Staff Management: 8+ years directing large operations teams to perform at a high level.
```

## Section 4: Areas of Expertise / Key Skills

**Strategy:** Leave this blank until after writing all bullet points. Two reasons:
1. Provides flexibility for spacing (add/remove skills to fill/trim page)
2. Writing bullets may surface additional skills you want to list

**Target:** 10–16 skills | 1–3 words each | Pulled from job posting keyword list

**Format options:**
- Simple two-column or three-column grid with check marks (✓)
- Comma-separated list
- Simple line-by-line list

**Example:**
```
KEY SKILLS
✓ Regulation Compliance   ✓ Process Mapping       ✓ Staff Scheduling
✓ Budget Management       ✓ Logistical Planning   ✓ Vendor Negotiations
✓ Lean Operations         ✓ Cost Analyses         ✓ Change Leadership
```

## Section 5: Professional Experience — Each Role

**Structure per role:**
```
[Job Title] | [Company Name] | [City, State] | [Month YYYY – Month YYYY]

[2–3 line job overview — context ONLY, no accomplishments]

• [Badass Bullet Point — accomplishment]
• [Badass Bullet Point — accomplishment]
• [Badass Bullet Point — accomplishment]
(3–6 bullets per role is typical)
```

**Job Overview Formula:**
> "Reporting to the [Title], the role includes [brief summary of core responsibilities] for [type/size of organization]."

**Example:**
> "Reporting to the VP of Revenue, the role includes coordinating the warehouse and inventory ops team of 30 to facilitate efficient receiving, storage, and delivery fulfillment for the flooring solution provider."

**Date rule:** Always include months (June 2019 – March 2023). Omitting months hides short tenures but savvy recruiters notice. Degrees over 15 years old: omit the year.

---

## Section 6: Education

**Always include:**
- Full credential name (B.A. abbreviations fine)
- School / governing body
- Year (omit for degrees 15+ years old)
- Location (if space allows)

**Add only if graduated within 2 years:**
- Impressive GPA (optional)
- Relevant courses
- Capstone project details
- Exchange programs
- Thesis (always include regardless of graduation year)

**Incomplete degree:** List field of study + "2 years completed" if it's your only post-secondary education.

**Training & Certifications:** Either append to Education section or create a separate "Certifications" section.

---

## Section 7: Volunteer Experience

**Type 1 — "I was working but unpaid"** (student clubs, complex responsibilities):
- Treat exactly like a job: title, org, dates, overview, bullet points
- Students/recent grads: include under "Relevant Experience" heading

**Type 2 — "I'm a good person"** (cause-based volunteering):
- One-line bullet under a Volunteer Experience heading
- Example: `Canadian Cancer Society Relay for Life Coordinator | 2010–Present`
  - Led committee to plan and deliver 10 events raising over $4M cumulatively.

---

## Section 8: Additional Information (Optional)

Include any of these if they add value:
- Languages (specify proficiency level)
- Professional memberships / associations
- Publications or patents
- Awards and recognition
- Relevant side projects or freelance work

## The 5-5-5 Method (Quick Resume Refresh)

**For existing resumes that need a fast upgrade:**

1. **Add a professional title** under your name (2–5 words) — biggest single-change impact
2. **Add numbers to bullets** — "Planned and executed concerts" → "Planned and executed 6 concerts attended by 20,000+ participants"
3. **Bold your best phrases** — identify the most impressive segment of each top bullet and bold it

Takes 5 minutes. Makes the resume 5x more noticeable.

## Resume No-No's (Langstaff's List)

- Objective statements (outdated — use Professional Summary instead)
- Inconsistent formatting (mixed date formats, varied indentation)
- First person language in any branding section
- Duties-only bullets with no results
- Generic skills everyone lists ("Microsoft Office," "Team player")
- Font below 10pt or above 12pt for body text
- Resume over 2 pages for under 15 years experience (2 pages is fine at senior levels)
- Same resume submitted to every job without keyword tailoring
- Personal information (age, marital status, photo in US/Canada)
- Email addresses that are unprofessional (e.g., hottie1985@...)
