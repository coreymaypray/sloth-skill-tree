# LinkedIn Optimization — The Core Four + All-Star System (Langstaff)

## Why LinkedIn Matters More Than Ever

- Recruiters make a hiring decision in **1–2 seconds** based on your profile
- All-Star profile status = **40x more opportunities**
- 5+ skills listed = **33x more recruiter contact** + 17x more profile views
- **Open to Work** badge = 40% increase in InMail messages
- Only 1% of LinkedIn users post weekly — yet they generate 9 billion impressions

## The Core Four (What Recruiters See First)

### 1. Profile Photo
- Professional attire (industry-appropriate — don't over-dress for casual fields)
- Direct eye contact with camera
- Simple, uncluttered background (solid color or soft blur)
- Good lighting — natural light or ring light
- Cropped to head and shoulders
- **No**: group photos, vacation photos, cropped-out partners, sunglasses, old photos

### 2. Banner Image
- The strip behind your photo — default gradient = missed branding opportunity
- Build in **Canva** (LinkedIn Banner template, 1584 × 396px)
- Include: name/title, your value prop or tagline, contact info or website, professional visual
- Match color palette and tone to your industry and personal brand

### 3. Headline (220 characters)
**Formula:** `[Role] | [Value Prop] | [Key Skills] | [Differentiator]`

**By career level:**

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Mid-Level | `Data Analyst | SQL, Python & Tableau | Turning Data into Decisions | 3x Analytics Award Winner` |
| Director | `Marketing Director | Brand Strategy & Growth | B2B SaaS Specialist | $50M+ Revenue Generated` |
| VP | `VP of Operations | Supply Chain Transformation | P&L Leader | Fortune 500 Experience` |
| C-Suite | `Chief Revenue Officer | GTM Strategy | Scaling B2B Companies from $10M to $100M ARR` |

**Rules:**
- Don't just list your job title — that's what everyone else does
- Include keywords recruiters search for
- Use pipe characters (|) as separators
- Make every word earn its spot

### 4. About Section (2,600 character max)
- **First ~300 characters** are visible before "See more" — this is your hook
- **Target length:** 1,800–2,200 characters (enough to be substantive, not wall-of-text)
- **Write in first person** (unlike resume summary)
- **Structure:**
  1. Opening hook (who you are and what you do in 2–3 sentences)
  2. What you're known for / your expertise areas
  3. Notable achievements with numbers
  4. Industries/clients/problems you specialize in
  5. Values or passion statement (brief)
  6. Call to action: "Open to connecting with..." or "Reach out at email@email.com"

## Skills Section Strategy

- **Target:** 20–30 skills listed
- Pull directly from your keyword master list (same as resume)
- Take **LinkedIn Skills Assessments** for top skills — verified badges appear on profile
- Assessments that pass = LinkedIn badge on profile = recruiter filter advantage
- List in order of relevance to target roles (most important first)
- Ask colleagues to endorse top 3–5 skills (reciprocate)

## Open to Work Settings

| Setting | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| **Recruiters Only** | Current employees who don't want employer to see (discreet) |
| **All LinkedIn Members** | Job seekers who don't mind visibility, want maximum reach |

Open to Work = 40% increase in InMail messages. If you're actively looking, turn it on.

## Experience Section

- Mirror your resume structure (title, company, dates, overview, bullet points)
- **But:** LinkedIn allows more space — can go slightly deeper on context
- Populate every role, not just recent ones
- Add media: presentations, links, portfolio pieces, project images

## Recommendations

- **Aim for:** 3–5 recommendations from managers and senior colleagues
- **How to get them:** Give first — write a recommendation for them, then ask
- **What makes a strong recommendation:** Specific accomplishment + your character + endorsement of capabilities for target role

## The Thought Leadership "3 Ps"

For senior roles, hiring decisions at Director+ are increasingly influenced by your LinkedIn presence before you even apply.

| P | Action | Frequency |
|---|--------|-----------|
| **Profile** | Optimized and complete | One-time + quarterly refresh |
| **Publishing** | Posts, articles, or reposts with commentary | 2–3x per week |
| **Participation** | Comments on others' posts, endorsements, reactions | Daily (5–10 min) |

**What to post:**
- Industry insights or trends you're tracking
- Lessons from recent projects (no confidential details)
- Reactions to news with your professional take
- Career advice or frameworks you've found useful
- Wins from your team (credit others publicly)

**Engagement compound effect:** Comments on high-traffic posts expose you to the poster's entire network — often faster follower/connection growth than original posting alone.

## LinkedIn vs. Resume: Key Differences

| Element | Resume | LinkedIn |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Summary voice | Third person | First person |
| Length | Tight (1–2 pages) | More room — use it |
| Photo | Never (US/Canada) | Essential |
| Audience | One hiring manager | Entire professional network + recruiters |
| Purpose | Get the interview | Get found + build credibility |
| Updates | Per application | Ongoing / evergreen |
