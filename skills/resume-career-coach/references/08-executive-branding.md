# Executive Branding — Director, VP, and C-Suite Positioning

## Regular Resume vs. Executive Resume: The Fundamental Shift

| Dimension | Regular Resume (IC / Manager) | Executive Resume (Director+) |
|-----------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Focus** | Tasks and responsibilities | Transformation and enterprise impact |
| **Metrics** | Individual performance metrics | P&L ownership, revenue, market share, valuation |
| **Tone** | Professional | Authoritative and visionary |
| **Scope** | Team or project | Enterprise-wide / cross-functional / board-level |
| **Length** | 1–2 pages | 2–3 pages (C-suite may go longer) |
| **Key question answered** | "What did you do?" | "What did the organization become because of you?" |

---

## The Korn Ferry 10% Rule

> At senior levels, **your resume is only 10% of your job search.**

The other 90%:
- LinkedIn presence and thought leadership
- Warm introductions through trusted networks
- Executive recruiter relationships
- Board member and investor visibility
- Speaking, publishing, advisory roles

**Practical implication:** If you're at Director+ and your search isn't working, the resume is rarely the problem. Start with network activation and LinkedIn optimization first.

---

## The Spencer Stuart Scope Specificity Rule

Every executive bullet must answer all of these:
- **Geography:** What region / countries / markets?
- **Business unit:** Which division, brand, or segment?
- **Revenue / budget size:** P&L ownership in dollars
- **Team size:** Direct reports + total org size
- **Time frame:** How long did it take?

**"P&L holder" is a highly abused term.** Without specificity, it means nothing to a sophisticated recruiter.

**Weak:** "P&L holder responsible for growing the business."
**Strong:** "Full P&L ownership for $380M North American enterprise software division (2,400 FTEs); grew EBITDA margin from 11% to 19% over 36 months through SG&A rationalization and portfolio optimization."

## Four Executive UVP Frameworks

### Framework 1 — Steve Blank (Positioning Statement)
> "I help **[type of organizations]** achieve **[specific outcome]** by **[your unique approach or method]**."

Example:
> "I help mid-market SaaS companies achieve category leadership by building go-to-market machines that turn product-market fit into scalable, repeatable revenue."

---

### Framework 2 — Geoffrey Moore (Crossing the Chasm)
> "For **[employer type]** who needs **[challenge addressed]**, I am a **[role]** who delivers **[specific benefit]**. Unlike **[typical alternative]**, I **[key differentiator]**."

Example:
> "For growth-stage B2B companies who need to cross the chasm from founder-led sales to institutional revenue, I am a CRO who delivers repeatable GTM infrastructure. Unlike operators who optimize existing systems, I build the playbook from scratch."

---

### Framework 3 — Power Tagline (Why-Buy-ROI)
A one-line brand statement that leads the resume headline or LinkedIn headline.

Examples:
- "Profit & Market Growth Architect | 3× Valuation Creator"
- "Global Supply Chain Transformer | $2B+ Savings Delivered"
- "B2B Revenue Engine Builder | $0 to $100M ARR in 4 Years"

**How to develop yours:**
1. List your top 10 career achievements
2. Find the recurring pattern: What do you do better than anyone?
3. Quantify it once, powerfully
4. Compress into 8–12 words

---

### Framework 4 — Evidence-Based UVP
Grounded entirely in specific, verifiable outcomes.

> "I have a track record of taking organizations from [starting state] to [end state] within [time frame], as evidenced by [3 specific examples]."

Example:
> "I have a track record of transforming underperforming revenue organizations into top-quartile performers within 18–24 months — evidenced by leading three consecutive turnarounds, each achieving 40–65% revenue growth and two resulting in successful acquisition exits."

## Executive Resume: Section-by-Section Adjustments

### Name + Titles
- Title 1: Your most senior / authoritative title or your personal brand tagline
- Title 2: Area of specialization or the role you're targeting

### Executive Summary (5–7 sentences for C-suite)
- Opens with scope (industries, company stages, geographies you've led)
- States your primary area of transformation expertise
- Includes 1–2 headline metrics
- Notes board/advisory experience if any
- Closes with the type of challenge you're built for

### Career Highlights (3–5 items at executive level)
Each highlight should represent a distinct type of value:
- Financial value (revenue, P&L, cost savings, valuation creation)
- Organizational value (scale, culture, talent)
- Market value (share, expansion, competitive position)
- Transformation value (turnaround, digital, M&A integration)

### Board, Advisory & Affiliations (new section for senior roles)
- Board memberships (for-profit and nonprofit)
- Advisory roles
- Industry council memberships
- Speaking engagements or publications (if prolific)

## Executive-Level Scope Statements (Templates)

Use these to open bullet points with enterprise context:

- "Overseeing $[X]M P&L with [N] direct reports across [X] business units..."
- "Leading [X]-person global organization spanning [N] countries and $[X]M in revenue..."
- "As the executive sponsor for [initiative] with $[X]M budget and board-level visibility..."
- "Charged by the CEO to [mission] within [time frame]..."

## Executive Job Search: What's Different

| Activity | IC / Manager | Director+ |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Resume importance | High | Medium (10% per Korn Ferry) |
| LinkedIn visibility | Important | Critical |
| Recruiter relationships | Nice to have | Essential |
| Network introductions | Helpful | Primary source |
| Board/advisory visibility | Not applicable | High leverage |
| Thought leadership content | Optional | Expected |
| Search timeline | 1–3 months typical | 3–9 months typical |

**Executive search firm relationships to build:**
- Spencer Stuart, Korn Ferry, Heidrick & Struggles (retained, top-tier)
- Russell Reynolds, Egon Zehnder (global board + C-suite)
- Functional boutiques (industry-specific firms place faster at VP level)

**How to get on their radar:**
- LinkedIn — well-optimized profile is the inbound channel
- Warm introductions from mutual connections
- Speaking at industry conferences
- Publishing on LinkedIn or industry publications
