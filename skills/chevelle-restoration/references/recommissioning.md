# Recommissioning a 1972 Chevelle After 3 Years of Storage

Complete checklist for safely bringing a classic car back to life after extended storage. Do NOT just turn the key — work through this systematically.

## Before Turning the Key

### 1. Battery
- [ ] Old battery is almost certainly dead — replace or load-test
- [ ] Clean battery tray for corrosion (baking soda + water, wire brush)
- [ ] Clean cable terminals to bright metal
- [ ] Install battery, connect positive first, then negative
- [ ] Verify 12.4V+ with multimeter before cranking

### 2. Engine Oil
- [ ] Check oil level — if low, do NOT just top off
- [ ] **Drain and replace** oil + filter regardless of mileage (moisture accumulates in old oil)
- [ ] Use conventional oil for break-in/restart: 10W-30 or 10W-40
- [ ] Oil filter: Wix, AC Delco, or NAPA Gold (standard Chevy small/big block filter)
- [ ] After refilling, do NOT start the engine yet — continue checklist

### 3. Coolant System
- [ ] Drain old coolant from radiator petcock AND block drain plugs
- [ ] Inspect radiator hoses (squeeze test — should be firm, not spongy or cracked)
- [ ] Inspect heater hoses
- [ ] Check thermostat housing for corrosion
- [ ] Inspect water pump weep hole for mineral deposits (indicates prior leak)
- [ ] Flush with garden hose until water runs clear
- [ ] Refill with 50/50 coolant mix (green conventional for iron block)
- [ ] Leave radiator cap off for first start (check for flow when thermostat opens)

### 4. Fuel System (Critical — #1 Failure Point)
- [ ] **Old gas is varnished** — do NOT try to run the engine on 3-year-old fuel
- [ ] Drain fuel tank (siphon or disconnect fuel line at tank)
- [ ] Inspect tank for rust/sediment. If rusty, remove and have cleaned/sealed (POR-15 tank sealer)
- [ ] Replace fuel filter (inline, near carburetor)
- [ ] If mechanical fuel pump: disconnect fuel line at carb, crank engine to verify pump delivers fuel
- [ ] Inspect rubber fuel lines for cracking — replace any suspect sections
- [ ] If carburetor was not stored with fuel stabilizer: rebuild or at minimum disassemble and clean jets, float bowl, needle/seat
- [ ] Refill with fresh premium fuel (higher octane helps with aged carb passages)
- [ ] Add fuel stabilizer to new fuel

### 5. Brakes (Safety Critical)
- [ ] Check master cylinder fluid level — brake fluid absorbs moisture over time
- [ ] **Flush and replace ALL brake fluid** (DOT 3 or DOT 4)
- [ ] Inspect brake lines (steel lines for rust pitting, rubber flex hoses for cracks/bulging)
- [ ] Inspect front brake pads/shoes (if drums) for glazing or crumbling
- [ ] Inspect rear brake shoes for glazing
- [ ] Check wheel cylinders for leaks (pull back dust boots, look for fluid)
- [ ] Check brake drums/rotors for deep scoring or heat spots
- [ ] Bleed brakes at all four wheels (start farthest from master cylinder: RR, LR, RF, LF)
- [ ] Pump brake pedal — should be firm, not spongy. If spongy, bleed again or check for leaks.
- [ ] Test parking brake cable for free movement

### 6. Transmission Fluid
- [ ] **Automatic (TH350/TH400)**: Check fluid level with engine running, in Park. Replace if dark/burnt smell.
- [ ] **Manual (Muncie/Saginaw)**: Check fill plug on side of trans. Top off with GL-4 gear oil (80W-90).
- [ ] Inspect for leaks at pan gasket, front seal, rear seal

### 7. Differential / Rear End
- [ ] Check fluid level at fill plug (rear of diff housing)
- [ ] Top off or replace with GL-5 gear oil (80W-90 or 75W-90)
- [ ] If posi-traction: use posi additive or posi-specific fluid

### 8. Power Steering (If Equipped)
- [ ] Check PS fluid level
- [ ] Inspect PS hoses for leaks, cracks
- [ ] Turn wheel lock-to-lock slowly a few times before starting engine

### 9. Belts & Hoses
- [ ] Inspect all drive belts for cracking, glazing, fraying
- [ ] Replace any questionable belt — they're cheap insurance
- [ ] Check belt tension (1/2" deflection at longest span)

## First Start Procedure

### Pre-Start Checks
- [ ] Oil is fresh and at proper level
- [ ] Coolant is filled, radiator cap OFF
- [ ] Battery is charged and connected
- [ ] Fuel system has fresh fuel and new filter
- [ ] All belts are in place and tensioned

### Starting
1. **Prime the oil system first**: Remove distributor cap, use a long flat-blade screwdriver or oil pump priming tool in a drill. Spin the oil pump shaft until you see oil pressure on the gauge (or oil at the valve cover). This prevents dry-start damage.
2. Reinstall distributor cap
3. Turn key to ON (not START) — listen for fuel pump (if electric) or verify no warning lights stuck
4. Crank engine — it may take 10-30 seconds of cranking with fresh fuel to fire
5. If it doesn't fire in 30 seconds: STOP. Check for spark at plugs, verify fuel delivery at carb
6. Once running: **immediately check oil pressure** — if no oil pressure, SHUT OFF immediately
7. Let idle for 2-3 minutes, watch temperature gauge
8. Verify coolant flow when thermostat opens (visible flow in radiator with cap off)
9. Install radiator cap once thermostat has opened and air is purged
10. Check for leaks: oil, coolant, fuel, trans fluid, PS fluid

### First Drive
- [ ] Drive slowly around the block first
- [ ] Test brakes at LOW speed before going further
- [ ] Listen for unusual noises: grinding, clunking, squealing
- [ ] Watch temperature gauge — should stabilize at ~195°F
- [ ] Check gauges frequently for the first 20-30 miles
- [ ] Re-torque wheel lug nuts after 25-50 miles (85-100 ft-lbs, star pattern)

## Tires

- [ ] Check tire age — tires over 6 years old should be replaced regardless of tread depth (check DOT date code on sidewall: last 4 digits = week/year of manufacture)
- [ ] After 3 years sitting, tires may have flat spots — may feel vibration initially
- [ ] Set tire pressures to spec (typically 28-32 PSI for vintage 14" or 15" tires)
- [ ] Inspect sidewalls for dry rot cracks

---

## Exhaust

- [ ] Inspect exhaust manifolds for cracks (common on big block)
- [ ] Check exhaust pipes and mufflers for rust-through
- [ ] Verify hangers are intact
- [ ] Start engine and check for exhaust leaks (listen/feel at joints)

---

## Ignition System

- [ ] Replace spark plugs (AC Delco R44T or equivalent, gapped to .035")
- [ ] Replace or inspect distributor cap and rotor
- [ ] Check plug wires for cracking or arcing
- [ ] Set timing with timing light once engine is running (varies by engine — typically 8-12° BTDC initial)
- [ ] If points ignition: Replace points and condenser, set dwell (28-32°)
- [ ] If HEI: Inspect pickup coil and module

---

## After First 100 Miles

- [ ] Re-check all fluid levels
- [ ] Re-torque lug nuts
- [ ] Inspect for new leaks (especially at gaskets and hose connections)
- [ ] Check battery voltage while running (should be 13.5-14.5V)
- [ ] Adjust idle speed if needed (typically 650-750 RPM in Drive for auto)
- [ ] Check and adjust choke operation if carburetor-equipped
