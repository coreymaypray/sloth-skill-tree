# Hook Writing

## Overview

Write scroll-stopping hooks, opening lines, headlines, and openers across all platforms for Corey Maypray's content ecosystem.

## Hook Types

### 1. Stat Hook
Lead with a surprising number or data point.
- "87% of breaches start with a phishing email. Your team clicked the last one."

### 2. Contrarian Hook
Challenge a common belief.
- "Zero Trust isn't a product. Stop buying it like one."

### 3. Story Hook
Open with a micro-narrative.
- "Last Tuesday, a client called me at 2AM. Their entire network was encrypted."

### 4. Question Hook
Ask something the reader can't scroll past.
- "What would happen to your business if every password leaked tomorrow?"

### 5. Bold Claim Hook
Make a provocative statement you can back up.
- "AI won't replace your security team. But a team using AI will replace yours."

### 6. Pain Point Hook
Name the exact frustration your audience feels.
- "You hired a CISO. Your board still doesn't understand your risk posture."

### 7. Time-Pressure Hook
Create urgency without being clickbait.
- "The average dwell time for ransomware is 11 days. Day 1 was yesterday."

## Platform Guidelines

| Platform | Max Length | Style |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| LinkedIn | 2 lines before "see more" | Professional but punchy |
| X/Twitter | 280 chars | Sharp, quotable |
| TikTok/Reels | 3-5 seconds spoken | Conversational, direct |
| YouTube Title | 60 chars | Curiosity + clarity |
| Email Subject | 50 chars | Personal, specific |
| Blog/Article | 1-2 sentences | Narrative or stat-driven |

## Rules

- Never use: "In this post I'll...", "Have you ever wondered...", "Let's talk about..."
- Every hook must pass the "would I stop scrolling?" test
- Match the voice to the platform and audience
- Generate 5-10 options per request, ranked by strength
- Include the hook type label for each option
- If the topic is cybersecurity, use specific technical details (not vague fear)
